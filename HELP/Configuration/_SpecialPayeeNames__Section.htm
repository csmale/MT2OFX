[SpecialPayeeNames] Section

 

SpecialPayeeNames is only used for ABN AMRO and will be replaced soon by functionality in the processing script!

 

The section is used to provide "Payee" names for transactions which by their nature do not have one. For example, cash withdrawals, bank charges and card costs do not have a "Payee" in the normal sense.

 

The reason for wanting to derive a "Payee" for these transactions, is that software such as Microsoft Money displays the payee prominently to allow you to identify the transaction. Seeing your own name or the name of your bank is not always helpful to you; hence this facility.

 

Each transaction has its memo field checked against the patterns defined here. On the first match, the payee string associated with the matching pattern is selected and used as the "Payee". Comparisons are case-insensitive and are deemed to begin at the start of the memo string.

 

In the following example, a transaction with a memo field of "Chipknip 10 Juli" will be given a payee of "Electronic Purse".

 

[SpecialPayeeNames]

Pattern1=GELDAUTOMAAT

Payee1=Cash Withdrawal

Pattern2=CHIPKNIP

Payee2=Electronic Purse

Pattern3=ELECTRONIC BANKING

Payee3=Bank

Pattern4=KOSTEN

Payee4=Bank

Pattern5=(JAAR)KOSTEN

Payee5=Bank

Pattern6=AFSLUITING PER

Payee6=Interest

Pattern7=RENTEAFSLUITING

Payee7=Interest

 

Technical note: The comparisons are performed with the Visual Basic "Like" operator. You can use a question mark ("?") to match any single character, or an asterisk ("*") to match any sequence of characters.
